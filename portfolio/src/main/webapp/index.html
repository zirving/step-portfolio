Who am I?

I am a junior year student at Loyola Marymount University in Los Angeles. I am studying Computer Science and Economics, with minors in Philosophy and Mathematics.

My professional interests are varied but are united around a central mission of using data and logical methods to find solutions to complex problems. I enjoy coming up with an algorithmic solution to a problem that would be impossible for a person to solve. Programming has been one of my primary ways to scratch this problem solving itch.

I also enjoy learning and teaching. Usually I am reading at least two books at a time, which leads to me finding more and more things I don't know enough about, creating a never ending cycle. Teaching is the other side of learning for me, and I have been a tutor either profressionally or casually for over five years.

Outside of work, I love anything to do with the outdoors. Hiking and camping are some of my favorite ways to get back to nature, and I love the water, enjoy time at the beach, swimming, and wakeboarding. Music is also a passion of mine, and I am always seeking out good music, both new and old. I also play the guitar, and am slowly improving.
